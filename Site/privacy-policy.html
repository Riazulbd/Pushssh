Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 18, 2026

1. Introduction & Scope

Ai Automation Flow, Inc. ("Company", "we", "us", "our", or "Ai Automation Flow") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our AI-powered voice and SMS communication platform (the "Service").

This Privacy Policy applies to our website, mobile applications, and all services provided through our platform. If you do not agree with our privacy practices, please do not use our Service.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Information You Provide Directly

Account Information: When you sign up for our Service, we collect your name, email address, phone number, company name, billing address, and payment information.

Configuration Data: Information about your business needs, such as contact lists, communication preferences, CRM integration details, and scripting preferences.

Communications Content: The text of SMS messages, call recordings, transcripts, and any content transmitted through our Service.

2.2 Recipient Information

You may provide us with personal information about recipients (contacts) when using our Service. We collect and process this information on your behalf to deliver communications. This includes names, phone numbers, email addresses, and any other information you choose to include.

2.3 Automatically Collected Information

Usage Data: We automatically collect information about how you interact with our Service, including IP addresses, browser type, operating system, referring URLs, pages visited, time and date stamps, and clickstream data.

Device Information: Information about the devices you use to access our Service, including device type, operating system, and unique device identifiers.

Cookies and Tracking: We use cookies, web beacons, and similar tracking technologies to enhance your experience and understand how you use our Service.

Location Data: We may collect approximate location information based on IP address, with your consent for more precise location data.

2.4 Third-Party Information

We may receive information from third-party services you authorize us to integrate with, such as your CRM system, calendar application, or email provider.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use collected information for the following purposes:

  • To provide, maintain, and improve our Service
  • To process transactions and send billing information
  • To deliver communications on your behalf through our AI agents
  • To authenticate users and provide account security
  • To send service notifications and support communications
  • To respond to your inquiries and provide customer support
  • To personalize and improve user experience
  • To analyze usage patterns and optimize our Service
  • To detect, prevent, and address fraud and security issues
  • To comply with legal obligations and law enforcement requests
  • To conduct research and analytics (in anonymized or aggregated form)
  • To send marketing communications with your consent
  • To facilitate integrations with third-party services

4. Data Security & Compliance

We implement industry-standard security measures to protect your information from unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. These measures include:

  • 256-bit encryption for data in transit and at rest
  • Secure authentication and access controls
  • Regular security audits and penetration testing
  • Employee training on data protection and privacy
  • Incident response procedures

4.1 Compliance Certifications

Our Service is compliant with:

  • HIPAA: For customers in healthcare requiring protected health information handling
  • SOC 2 Type II: Third-party audited security and operational effectiveness
  • TCPA Compliance: Telephone Consumer Protection Act requirements for consent and opt-out management
  • GDPR: General Data Protection Regulation requirements for EU users
  • CCPA: California Consumer Privacy Act requirements for California residents

5. Data Retention

We retain your information for as long as necessary to provide our Service and comply with legal obligations. Specific retention periods include:

  • Call Recordings: Retained for 90 days by default; longer retention available upon request
  • Transcripts: Retained indefinitely unless you request deletion
  • Account Data: Retained while your account is active and for 30 days after termination
  • Usage Logs: Retained for 12 months for compliance and security purposes

You can request deletion of specific data at any time, subject to legal retention requirements.

6. Data Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell your personal information. We may share information in the following circumstances:

  • Third-Party Service Providers: We share data with vendors who assist in providing our Service (hosting, analytics, payment processing) under data processing agreements
  • CRM and Integrations: Data is shared with your integrated CRM systems as you configure
  • Legal Requirements: We may disclose information to comply with law, court orders, or government requests
  • Business Transfers: In case of merger, acquisition, or bankruptcy, your information may be transferred as part of the transaction
  • With Your Consent: We may share information when you explicitly authorize us to do so

7. International Data Transfers

Our Service is provided from the United States. If you access our Service from outside the US, you consent to the transfer of your information to the United States and other countries where we operate. We implement appropriate safeguards for international transfers, including Standard Contractual Clauses where required by GDPR.

8. Your Privacy Rights

8.1 General Rights

You have the right to:

  • Access your personal information
  • Correct inaccurate information
  • Request deletion of your information
  • Opt-out of marketing communications
  • Withdraw consent at any time

8.2 GDPR Rights (EU Users)

If you are in the European Union, you have additional rights under GDPR:

  • Right to Access: Obtain a copy of your personal data
  • Right to Rectification: Correct inaccurate data
  • Right to Erasure: Request deletion of your data ("right to be forgotten")
  • Right to Restrict Processing: Limit how we use your data
  • Right to Data Portability: Receive your data in a structured format
  • Right to Object: Oppose processing of your data
  • Right to Lodge a Complaint: File a complaint with your local data protection authority

8.3 CCPA Rights (California Residents)

If you are a California resident, you have rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act:

  • Right to Know: What personal information is collected, used, and shared
  • Right to Delete: Request deletion of personal information collected
  • Right to Opt-Out: Opt-out of the sale or sharing of personal information
  • Right to Correct: Correct inaccurate personal information
  • Right to Non-Discrimination: We will not discriminate against you for exercising your rights
