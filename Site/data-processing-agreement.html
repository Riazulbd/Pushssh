Data Processing Agreement

Last updated: April 18, 2026

1. Scope and Application

This Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") is entered into between Ai Automation Flow, Inc. ("Data Processor" or "Processor") and the Customer ("Data Controller" or "Controller"). This DPA applies to the processing of personal data by the Processor on behalf of the Controller in connection with the provision of the Service.

This DPA supplements the Terms and Conditions and Privacy Policy. To the extent there is any conflict between this DPA and those documents, this DPA shall prevail with respect to data processing activities.

2. Definitions

The following definitions apply to this Agreement:

  • Personal Data: Any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person.
  • Processing: Any operation performed on personal data, including collection, recording, organization, structuring, storage, adaptation, retrieval, use, disclosure, transmission, erasure, or destruction.
  • Data Controller: The natural or legal person who determines the purposes and means of processing personal data.
  • Data Processor: The natural or legal person who processes personal data on behalf of the Data Controller.
  • Sub-processor: Any natural or legal person engaged by the Processor to process personal data on behalf of the Controller.
  • Recipient: A natural or legal person to whom personal data is disclosed.
  • GDPR: Regulation (EU) 2016/679 of the European Parliament and Council on the protection of natural persons with regard to the processing of personal data.

3. Nature and Scope of Processing

The Controller authorizes the Processor to process personal data for the following purposes:

  • Providing voice and SMS communication services
  • Processing call recordings, transcripts, and communications
  • Integrating with Customer's CRM and business systems
  • Providing analytics, reporting, and performance dashboards
  • Improving and maintaining the Service
  • Complying with legal and regulatory obligations

The categories of personal data processed include:

  • Contact information (names, phone numbers, email addresses)
  • Call recordings and transcripts
  • SMS message content
  • Communication metadata (timestamps, duration, parties involved)
  • CRM-related information provided by Customer

The categories of data subjects include end customers, leads, prospects, and individuals contacted through the Service.

4. Duration of Processing

The Processor shall process personal data for the duration of the Service Agreement and as long as necessary to fulfill obligations under applicable law, unless the Controller requests deletion in accordance with the terms of this DPA and the applicable law.

5. Processor Obligations

The Processor shall:

  • Process personal data only on documented instructions from the Controller
  • Ensure that persons authorized to process personal data are committed to confidentiality or under an appropriate legal obligation of confidentiality
  • Implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk
  • Assist the Controller in ensuring compliance with data subject rights requests
  • Assist the Controller in complying with Articles 32-36 of the GDPR (security, breach notification, impact assessments)
  • Delete or return all personal data after the end of the provision of Services, unless applicable law requires storage
  • Make available to the Controller all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with this DPA
  • Notify the Controller without undue delay if a data processing instruction is unlawful

6. Sub-processors

The Processor is authorized to engage sub-processors to process personal data. The Processor shall:

  • Obtain prior specific or general written authorization from the Controller before engaging sub-processors
  • Impose data protection obligations on sub-processors through contracts equivalent to those in this DPA
  • Inform the Controller of any intended changes regarding the addition or replacement of sub-processors, giving the Controller an opportunity to object
  • Remain liable for sub-processor performance to the same extent as if the Processor were performing the services

Current authorized sub-processors include hosting providers, analytics services, and CRM integration partners. A list of current sub-processors is available upon request.

7. Security Measures

The Processor implements and maintains appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect personal data against unauthorized or unlawful processing and against accidental loss, destruction, or damage. These measures include:

  • Encryption of personal data in transit and at rest using industry-standard protocols (TLS 1.2+, AES-256)
  • Access controls and authentication mechanisms (multi-factor authentication where applicable)
  • Regular security assessments, audits, and penetration testing
  • Employee training and awareness programs on data protection
  • Incident response and data breach procedures
  • Network security measures (firewalls, intrusion detection)
  • Physical security controls for data centers
  • Pseudonymization and encryption of sensitive data

The Processor shall maintain SOC 2 Type II certification and provide evidence of compliance upon request.

8. Data Subject Rights

The Processor shall, taking into account the nature of processing, assist the Controller by appropriate technical and organizational measures in fulfilling the Controller's obligation to respond to data subject rights requests, including:

  • Right of access (Article 15, GDPR)
  • Right to rectification (Article 16, GDPR)
  • Right to erasure (Article 17, GDPR)
  • Right to restrict processing (Article 18, GDPR)
  • Right to data portability (Article 20, GDPR)
  • Right to object (Article 21, GDPR)

The Processor shall respond to Controller requests regarding data subject rights within 5 business days.

9. Data Breach Notification

The Processor shall notify the Controller without undue delay and in no case later than 48 hours after becoming aware of a personal data breach. The notification shall include:

  • A description of the breach and its likely consequences
  • The name and contact details of the Data Protection Officer or other contact point
  • A description of the likely consequences of the breach
  • Measures taken or proposed to address the breach and mitigate harm

The Processor shall cooperate with the Controller in investigating the breach and complying with notification obligations to authorities and data subjects.

10. International Data Transfers

To the extent that the Processor transfers personal data outside of the European Union, the United Kingdom, or other jurisdictions with adequacy decisions, the Processor shall implement appropriate safeguards such as:

  • Standard Contractual Clauses (SCC) approved by the European Commission
  • Binding Corporate Rules (BCR)
  • Other mechanisms approved under applicable data protection laws

The Controller consents to international transfers necessary to provide the Service as described in the Terms and Conditions.

11. Audit and Inspection Rights

The Controller has the right to:

  • Audit and inspect the Processor's data protection practices
  • Receive copies of audit reports and certifications (SOC 2, ISO 27001, etc.)
  • Conduct on-site audits with reasonable notice
  • Request security assessments and penetration testing results

The Processor shall provide reasonable cooperation with audits, provided such audits do not unreasonably disrupt operations. Audits may be conducted no more than once per year unless required by law or following a data breach.

12. Data Deletion and Return

Upon termination or expiry of the Services, the Processor shall, at the Controller's choice:

  • Delete all personal data processed on behalf of the Controller
  • Return all personal data to the Controller in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format

The Processor may retain personal data to the extent required by applicable law, provided such data is handled in accordance with this DPA. Deletion shall be certified in writing to the Controller.

13. Compliance with Laws

The Processor shall comply with all applicable data protection laws and regulations, including GDPR, CCPA, HIPAA, and other regional privacy laws. The Processor shall:

  • Conduct Data Protection Impact Assessments where required
  • Document processing activities and maintain records of processing
  • Report to supervisory authorities when legally required
  • Maintain a Register of Processing Activities

14. Duration and Termination

This DPA shall remain in effect for the duration of the Service Agreement. Upon termination of the Service Agreement, the obligations of the Processor with respect to data deletion or return shall survive and remain in effect.

15. Modifications to the DPA

The Processor may modify this DPA to comply with changes in applicable law or to enhance data protection practices. The Processor shall provide the Controller with at least 30 days' notice of material changes and shall inform the Controller of the changes. The Controller may terminate the Service if material modifications are unacceptable.

16. Severability

If any provision of this DPA is found to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect. If a provision is found invalid, the parties shall work together in good faith to replace it with a valid provision that achieves the same objective.
