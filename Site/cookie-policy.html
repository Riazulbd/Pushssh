Cookie Policy

Last updated: April 18, 2026

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device when you visit our website or use our Service. These files contain information that allows us to recognize your device and remember your preferences. Cookies are widely used to improve user experience, understand how people use websites, and deliver personalized content.

2. How We Use Cookies

Ai Automation Flow uses cookies for several purposes:

2.1 Essential Cookies

These cookies are necessary for the basic functionality of our Service. They enable core features such as:

  • User authentication and login session management
  • Account security and fraud prevention
  • Remembering your preferences and settings
  • Maintaining your shopping cart or configuration selections
  • Protecting against cross-site request forgery (CSRF)

Essential cookies are required for the Service to function properly. You cannot opt out of these cookies without disabling core functionality.

2.2 Performance and Analytics Cookies

These cookies help us understand how you use our Service and improve user experience. They collect anonymized data about:

  • Pages you visit and features you use
  • Time spent on different pages
  • Clicks, scrolls, and interactions
  • Browser type and operating system
  • Referral sources and exit pages
  • Error tracking and performance monitoring

We use analytics providers such as Google Analytics to collect and analyze this data. Analytics cookies are used solely to improve our Service and understand user behavior patterns.

2.3 Marketing and Advertising Cookies

These cookies are used to deliver personalized advertising and marketing communications. They may:

  • Remember your interests and browsing activity
  • Display ads tailored to your interests across the web
  • Measure the effectiveness of advertising campaigns
  • Prevent showing you the same ads repeatedly
  • Track conversions from advertising campaigns

Marketing cookies are not essential and can be disabled through cookie preferences or your browser settings.

2.4 Third-Party Cookies

Our Service may include third-party services that use their own cookies, including:

  • Google Analytics: Web analytics and performance monitoring
  • Advertising Partners: For serving and tracking personalized ads
  • CRM Integrations: For synchronizing data with your CRM system
  • Payment Processors: For secure payment processing
  • Social Media Platforms: For social sharing and tracking

These third parties have their own privacy policies governing their use of cookies. We are not responsible for their cookie practices.

3. Specific Cookies We Use

The following table describes the main cookies used on our Service:

  • session_id: Maintains your login session (Essential, expires at session end)
  • user_preferences: Stores your selected preferences and settings (Essential, 1 year)
  • csrf_token: Prevents cross-site request forgery attacks (Essential, session duration)
  • _ga, _gid: Google Analytics cookies for traffic analysis (Analytics, 2 years)
  • __utmz, __utmc: Google Analytics tracking and campaign source (Analytics, session/2 years)
  • conversion_tracking: Tracks conversion actions for marketing attribution (Marketing, 90 days)
  • retargeting_id: Identifies users for retargeting ads (Marketing, 1 year)

4. How to Control Cookies

You have several options for controlling and managing cookies:

4.1 Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can typically:

  • Accept all cookies
  • Reject all cookies
  • Accept only certain types of cookies
  • Delete cookies from your device
  • Receive alerts when websites attempt to set cookies

Please note that disabling essential cookies may impair or disable functionality of our Service. For browser-specific instructions, visit:

  • Chrome: https://support.google.com/chrome/answer/95647
  • Firefox: https://support.mozilla.org/en-US/kb/enable-and-disable-cookies-website-preferences
  • Safari: https://support.apple.com/en-us/HT201265
  • Edge: https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/microsoft-edge/delete-cookies-in-microsoft-edge

4.2 Cookie Consent Preferences

When you first visit our Service, we display a cookie consent banner. You can manage your cookie preferences at any time by clicking the "Cookie Preferences" link in the footer of our website.

4.3 Do Not Track (DNT)

Some browsers include a Do Not Track feature. We currently do not respond to DNT signals, but we honor cookie preferences you set through our cookie consent mechanism.

4.4 Opt-Out of Analytics

To opt out of Google Analytics tracking, install the Google Analytics Opt-Out Browser Add-on at: https://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout

5. Cookies and GDPR

For users in the European Union, we comply with GDPR requirements regarding cookies:

  • We obtain your explicit consent before placing non-essential cookies
  • We provide clear information about cookies before consent is requested
  • You can withdraw consent at any time through cookie preferences
  • Essential cookies are placed without requiring consent
  • We maintain records of your cookie consent choices

6. Cookies and CCPA

For California residents, cookies may constitute "personal information" under the CCPA. Your cookie preferences and the ability to opt out of cookies are part of your privacy rights. You can control cookies through the mechanisms described in Section 4.

7. Flash Cookies and Similar Technologies

We may use Flash cookies (Local Shared Objects) or similar technologies to store information beyond standard browser cookies. These are used sparingly and only when necessary for Service functionality. You can manage Flash cookies through your Flash Player settings.

8. Pixel Tags and Web Beacons

In addition to cookies, we use pixel tags and web beacons to:

  • Track email opens and click-throughs
  • Measure the effectiveness of advertising campaigns
  • Understand how users navigate our website
  • Monitor conversion events

These are typically small, transparent images embedded in web pages or emails. You can disable them by disabling images in your browser, though this may affect the user experience.

9. Cookie Retention

We retain cookies only as long as necessary for their stated purpose:

  • Session Cookies: Deleted when you close your browser
  • Persistent Cookies: Retained from a few days to several years depending on purpose
  • Analytics Cookies: Typically retained for 2 years
  • Marketing Cookies: Typically retained for 90 days to 1 year

You can delete cookies at any time through your browser settings.
